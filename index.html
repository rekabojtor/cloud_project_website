Toy Poodle Guide for First-Time Owners

A friendly, colorful primer with quick tips, caring essentials, and cute photos to help you get started.

About toy poodles

Toy poodles are the smallest poodle variety — clever, affectionate, and lively. They typically weigh 4–6 kg (9–12 lbs) and can live 12–15 years or more. Their curly coat is low-shedding but needs regular care. Great for apartment life when given daily play and attention.

Training

  • Use short positive sessions (5–10 min) to keep them engaged.
  • Socialize with people and dogs early to build confidence.

Grooming

  • Brush several times a week to prevent mats; trims every 6–8 weeks.
  • Regular ear checks help avoid infections (their ears are floppy).

Practical tips

  • Measure food to avoid overfeeding—small dogs pack on weight fast.
  • Provide mental toys and short walks; they're energetic but not marathon runners.
  • Brush your puppy's teeth several times a week to prevent dental disease.
  • Use a harness rather than a collar for walks to protect their neck.

Fun facts

  • Poodles were originally water retrievers—surprising for such a small dog!
  • They come in many colors: apricot, cream, black, silver, and more.
  • Toy poodles excel at tricks and agility; their intelligence is a big plus.
